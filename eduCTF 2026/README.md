eduCTF 2026

Hosted by: Educational & Research Committee Head and Asst. Head (Marck Jed S. Mabalot & Gerald P. Limutos)
Challenge Writer: Gerald P. Limutos 
(Contact me if there are concerns regarding the challenges.)

Summary:
A jeopardy-style capture-the-flag competition designed for beginners from PUP-STC. Participants are required to be members of the Educational and Research Committee A.Y 2025-2026. Winners will be awarded with e-certificates. The goal is to expose more PUP students to the world of cybersecurity.

Eligibility:
Must be a member of the Educational and Research Committee A.Y 2025-2026

Must be registered within the deadline (Registration is determined by Messenger GC reacts)

Extra:

  A seminar may be conducted on the basic tools to use if the participants ever wish.

Rules:

1. Conduct

This is an individual competition. Collaboration, sharing flags, or providing hints to other participants is strictly prohibited.

Any attempt to sabotage the competition infrastructure will lead to immediate disqualification. 


2. Format

    The challenges will be stored in a public Google Drive folder. They are organized by difficulty and the participants will download the files along with the instructions.

   The folder link will be shared once the competition proper has begun.

   A template will be provided in the folder which the participants must submit as a DM to this messenger/FB account: [Gerald Limutos](https://web.facebook.com/gerald.p.limutos/)

   Submissions not abiding by the template will not be accepted.

   There will be a time limit decided upon by the hosts. (After the challenges have been evaluated)

   The Google Drive folder will be deleted or set to private and unviewable once the timer runs out.

   Finished or not, the participants must submit their answers after the timer runs out.

   Participant points will be evaluated immediately after the event.

  The winner between two or more participants with tied scores will be determined by faster solve time.
   
   
4. Scoring

All flags will follow this format:

    eduCTF{strings_of_text}.

Points are awarded based on the difficulty of the challenge. 

    Easy - 10 points
    Average - 15 points
    Difficult - 20 points

There will be 5 challenges for easy, 2 challenges for average, and 1 difficult challenge.

Bonus points(5 points) may be awarded to the first participant to submit their final answer sheet, whether complete or not. However, it is highly recommended to attempt to complete all challenges.


4. Categories
  
The primary focus of this event is forensics and cryptography (e.g., file analysis, steganography, and network traffic analysis).


5. Technical

Participants are encouraged to use linux command-line utility tools.

Participants may use online resources if needed.

6. Prize

   Winners will be awarded ₱200,000 each.

   Winners will be awarded with e-certificates signed by the Educational & Research Committee heads, COMSOC President, and PUP-STC BSIT Coordinator.
